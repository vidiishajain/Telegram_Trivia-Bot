# Problem

> **Worked example** — what a filled-in `docs/problem.md` looks like, for the terminal
> "agent idea generator" demo. (Stage 1: identify the limit of your current agency.)

A beginner who just cloned this starter often gets stuck at the very first step: **what
should I even build?** They have interests but not a concrete, exciting, *buildable* project
in mind, so they stall before writing a line of code.

Today they'd brainstorm alone or scroll tutorials — slow, and the ideas rarely fit *their*
interest or this toolkit. The limit isn't coding; it's **turning a vague interest into one
crisp, doable first project.**

## Why an agent

An LLM can do the part a blank page can't: take "I like cooking" and, grounded in a quick
web scan of what's current, hand back a specific, motivating idea scoped to the services
this starter provides. That's judgement + fresh context, not a lookup.
